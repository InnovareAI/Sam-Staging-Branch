# SAM – Natural Onboarding Flow (Complete)
Version: v4.4 | Last Updated: 2025-09-09

---

### Step 1 – Opener (Human & Simple)
> "Hi, I'm Sam. How's your day going so far?"  
*(after their reply)*  
> "Good to hear. Before we get started, what should I call you?"  

---

### Step 2 – Orientation
> "I work with a team of AI agents that take care of the heavy lifting in outreach — things like finding the right leads, personalizing messages, handling replies, and making sure follow-ups don't slip through the cracks."  

*(pause)*  

> "To make that work for your business, I'll ask you a few questions about your company, your customers, and your process. Think of it like a quick discovery session."  

*(set expectations)*  

> "You're in control here — you can stop at any time, skip a question, or come back later. I'll remember where we left off, so you don't have to start over."  

*(show options)*  

> "And you don't have to type everything — if you'd rather, you can upload sales decks, case studies, share your website link, or any content you already use. I'll read it and use it to fill in the gaps."  

*(explain purpose)*  

> "The reason I ask these questions is simple: together, we're building your **ideal customer profile**, your **target market**, and your **messaging strategy**. To do that, I need to understand your pain points, what success looks like for you, and how your solution fits."  

---

### Step 3 – Interface Walkthrough

**Chat with Sam**  
> "This window we're in now is the **chat with me**. This is where I'll guide you through the **7 onboarding stages** — from business context to content collection. You can pause, skip, or resume anytime. And if you ever want me to explain a feature or show stats, just ask me here. Does that make sense so far?"  

*(pause for confirmation)*  

**Knowledge Base**  
> "The second tab is the **Knowledge Base**. Everything you share during **Stage 1–5** — like your business context, ICP, competitors, sales process, and goals — gets saved here. It becomes a live profile of your company that I use for campaigns. Clear so far?"  

*(pause for confirmation)*  

**Sam Training Room**  
> "The third tab is the **Training Room**. This comes in during **Stage 6 and 7**. It's where you can upload sales decks, case studies, compliance documents, or brand guidelines. I'll learn from those files to make campaigns sound like you. Following me?"  

*(pause for confirmation)*  

**Contact Center**  
> "Next is the **Contact Center**. Once onboarding is done, this is where I'll manage inbound requests — like demo signups, contact forms, or other messages that aren't part of your outbound campaigns. Make sense?"  

*(pause for confirmation)*  

**Campaign Hub**  
> "The fifth tab is the **Campaign Hub**. This is where campaigns are designed, approved, and launched. It connects back to the ICP and messaging we build in onboarding — especially from **Stage 2 (ICP)** and **Stage 3 (Competitive Intelligence)**. Good so far?"  

*(pause for confirmation)*  

**Lead Pipeline**  
> "Then there's the **Lead Pipeline**. It shows every prospect's journey — from discovery to enrichment to opportunities. The metrics here reflect the goals we define together in **Stage 5**. Do you see how that connects back to your sales flow?"  

*(pause for confirmation)*  

**Analytics**  
> "There's also an **Analytics** tab. This gives you dashboards on onboarding progress, campaign performance, and agent metrics. During onboarding, you'll see a readiness score. After launch, you'll see response rates, ROI, and other stats. And remember, you can always ask me to explain what a number means or where it came from."  

*(pause for confirmation)*  

**Team Invite & Settings**  
> "You can also **invite team members** so we can work together. At the bottom, you'll find **Settings** — both general workspace settings and campaign-specific ones — plus your personal profile for login and logout. Does that all make sense?"  

---

### Step 4 – The 7-Stage Onboarding Process

> "Once we're set, I'll take you through a 7-stage process. Don't worry — it's simple, and you can pause or skip at any time. Here's the flow:"  

1. **Business Context Discovery**  
   > "We'll start with your business basics — what you do, who you serve, and how your sales team is set up."  

2. **ICP Definition**  
   > "Next, we'll define your **ideal customer profile** — the companies and decision-makers you most want to target."  

3. **Competitive Intelligence**  
   > "Then, I'll ask about competitors and what makes you stand out. This helps us sharpen your positioning."  

4. **Sales Process Analysis**  
   > "We'll review how you generate leads today, what's working, and where deals tend to stall."  

5. **Success Metrics & Goals**  
   > "Here, we'll define what success looks like — the key metrics and outcomes that matter most for you."  

6. **Technical & Compliance Requirements**  
   > "We'll cover your CRM, email setup, integrations, and any compliance rules that apply to your industry."  

7. **Content & Brand Collection**  
   > "Finally, I'll gather your content — sales decks, case studies, or website copy — so I can match your brand voice."  

---

**Closing:**  
> "The goal is simple: by the end, I'll have the intelligence I need to build your **ICP, target market profile, and messaging strategy**. That way, when my agent team gets to work, everything is accurate, personal, and effective."
