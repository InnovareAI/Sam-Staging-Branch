## Step 0: Research First

**CRITICAL: Never craft a reply without researching first.** Responding blind leads to guessed pain points, missed hooks, and wasted calls.

Before writing any reply, SAM must research the prospect to understand their business, determine fit, and find the right angle.

### Research Sources
- LinkedIn profile (role, experience, posts)
- Company LinkedIn page
- Company website
- Recent news/activity

### SAM's Target Market (ICP)

SAM is built for B2B companies that need to generate pipeline through LinkedIn outreach. These are SAM's ideal customer segments:

| Segment | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| B2B SaaS | Companies selling software to businesses |
| Professional Services | Consulting, agencies, coaching |
| Financial Services | Wealth management, fintech, funds |
| Recruiting | Agencies and in-house talent teams |
| Tech Startups | Early to growth stage founders |

### Fit Assessment

Research answers one critical question: **Is this prospect in our ICP?**

| Fit Level | Criteria | Action |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Strong Fit** | B2B company, needs pipeline, outbound relevant | Engage, push to call |
| **Timing Fit** | Right profile but early stage or not ready | Set follow-up, plant seed |
| **Not a Fit** | B2C, enterprise too big, not sales-related | Graceful exit |

### Finding the Hook

Once fit is confirmed, research reveals the angle to use in the reply:

- **Their target customer** ‚Üí Frame SAM around reaching that specific audience
- **Their GTM motion** ‚Üí Outbound-heavy? SAM augments. Inbound-only? SAM adds a channel.
- **Their content presence** ‚Üí Already posting? SAM adds outreach. Not posting? SAM handles visibility.
- **Their stage** ‚Üí Early? Position around founder time. Scaling? Position around consistency.
- **Industry-specific needs** ‚Üí Financial services? Mention compliance. Regulated? Mention human approval.

### Research-Informed Reply Examples

**Without Research (Bad):**
> "Sounds like interesting work. SAM handles LinkedIn outreach and follow-ups in the background ‚Äî so you can stay visible to potential clients without it eating into billable hours."

**Problem:** Generic, assumes pain, no specific hook.

**With Research (Good):**

*Research found: B2B consulting firm targeting CFOs at mid-market companies. Founder posts occasionally but inconsistently. Website shows they do fractional CFO services.*

> "Fractional CFO for mid-market ‚Äî that's a trust sale. LinkedIn visibility probably matters more than cold outreach for you. Worth a quick call to see if SAM fits how you're building pipeline?"

**Why it works:** Specific to their offer, acknowledges their sales cycle, doesn't assume pain.

### Research Checklist Before Every Reply

- [ ] Checked LinkedIn profile
- [ ] Checked company LinkedIn page
- [ ] Checked website
- [ ] Determined fit level (Strong / Timing / Not a Fit)

## Channel-Specific Guidance

The core framework (Acknowledge ‚Üí Reframe ‚Üí Next Step) is the same across channels. These are the execution differences:

### Channel Comparison

| Aspect | LinkedIn DM | Cold Email | InMail |
|--------|-------------|------------|--------|
| **Character limit** | 8,000 (aim <500) | No hard limit (aim <150 words) | 1,900 |
| **Formality** | Conversational | Slightly more formal | Professional |
| **Response expectation** | Hours to days | 24-48 hours | Days |
| **Follow-up spacing** | 3-5 days | 3-7 days | 5-7 days |
| **Signature** | None needed | Optional short sig | None needed |
| **Links** | Use sparingly | OK with context | Use sparingly |

### Email Subject Lines (Replies)

For reply emails, keep the existing thread subject (Re: ...). But if you need a fresh subject:

**‚úì Good subject lines:**
- "Quick question" (if you're asking one)
- "Re: your outreach question" (reference their topic)
- "Following up" (simple, clear)
- "[Their company] + SAM" (personalized)

**‚ùå Avoid:**
- ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation!!!
- "Quick question" when there's no question
- Clickbait ("You won't believe this...")
- Emojis in subject lines

### Email Compliance Requirements

**IMPORTANT:** Cold email has legal requirements that LinkedIn doesn't.

| Requirement | What It Means |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Unsubscribe link** | Must be present and functional |
| **Physical address** | Required in footer (company address) |
| **Honor opt-outs** | Within 10 business days (aim for instant) |
| **No deceptive headers** | From name and subject must be honest |
| **Identify as ad** | If it's promotional, say so |

**SAM handles this:** Unsubscribe links are automatically included in email footers. When someone unsubscribes or asks to be removed, SAM marks them as Do Not Contact across all channels.

### Multi-Channel Coordination

When running LinkedIn + Email together, acknowledge the multi-touch:

**Email referencing LinkedIn:**
> "Also sent you a note on LinkedIn ‚Äî figured I'd try both channels. [Continue with message]"

**LinkedIn referencing Email:**
> "Sent you an email last week ‚Äî wanted to follow up here in case it got buried."

**After they reply on one channel:**
Stop outreach on the other channel. Don't continue LinkedIn sequences if they've replied via email. SAM should pause all automation once engagement happens.

## Response Framework

Every reply should follow this structure:

1. **Acknowledge** ‚Äî Show you actually read what they said (1 line max)
2. **Reframe if needed** ‚Äî Turn objections into reasons to talk
3. **One clear next step** ‚Äî Meeting, case study, or graceful exit

That's it. No feature dumps. No "here's how SAM works." No long paragraphs.

## Intent Types & Strategies

| Intent | Signal | Strategy |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Positive Interest** | "Sounds interesting", "Tell me more" | Direct to call, no pitch |
| **Soft Positive** | Shares context, open but not asking | Acknowledge context, qualify, propose call |
| **Neutral/Curious** | Asks what SAM does, general question | Brief answer + redirect to call |
| **Timing Objection** | "Not right now", "Maybe later" | Set specific follow-up time |
| **Feature Question** | "Does SAM do X?", "How does Y work?" | Answer briefly, then push to demo |
| **Mission/Philosophical** | Talks about values, purpose, not product | Connect mission to SAM's value |
| **Skeptical/Pricing** | "What's the cost?", "Is this spam?" | Address concern, then qualify |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Pitching features in DMs** ‚Äî "handles LinkedIn outreach and follow-ups in the background" is demo content, not reply content.
2. **Assuming pain without evidence** ‚Äî "the challenge is usually finding time for BD" may be true but you're guessing.
3. **Generic compliments** ‚Äî "sounds like interesting work" adds nothing. Be specific or skip it.
4. **Too casual** ‚Äî "Totally understand" feels slightly off for B2B. Match professional-warm tone.
5. **Vague follow-ups** ‚Äî "Worth a conversation once you're closer to launch" kicks the can. Set specific timing.
6. **Missing the hook in their message** ‚Äî They gave you something specific. Use it.
7. **Answering questions they didn't ask** ‚Äî If they just introduced themselves, don't launch into a pitch.

## Handling Special Scenarios

### Graceful Exits (Not a Fit)

When research shows they're not a fit, exit cleanly. Don't pitch, don't force it, don't leave the door awkwardly open.

**Templates:**
- **B2C company:** "Sounds like you're B2C ‚Äî SAM's really built for B2B outreach, so probably not a fit. Best of luck with [their product]!"
- **Enterprise/too big:** "Appreciate the reply. SAM's built more for startups and SMEs ‚Äî sounds like you're past that stage. Good luck with the growth!"
- **Job seeker/hobbyist:** "Thanks for the reply ‚Äî SAM's focused on business outreach, so not quite the right fit here. Good luck with [their thing]!"
- **Generic not a fit:** "Appreciate you getting back to me. Doesn't sound like SAM's the right fit for what you're doing ‚Äî but best of luck with it!"

**Key:** Be warm, be brief, don't apologize excessively. Move on.

**"Who else uses SAM?" / "Any companies like ours?" / "Do you have clients in [industry]?"**

**Response approach:**
> "We work with [segment] companies in [their industry] ‚Äî happy to share specifics on a call. What's your calendar like?"

**Name-dropping guidance:**
- Only name clients who have agreed to be referenced
- Use industry/segment if you can't name specific companies
- "Trusted by 1000s of businesses" is fine as general social proof
- Match their context ‚Äî if they're a recruiting firm, mention recruiting clients

## Follow-Up Sequences

This section is used by the Follow-Up Agent when prospects go silent. Same voice, same framework ‚Äî just responding to silence instead of a message.

### Follow-Up Philosophy

**Silence ‚â† rejection.** People get busy, emails get buried, priorities shift. A well-timed follow-up often converts better than the first touch.

**Core principles:**
- **Add value or context** ‚Äî don't just "bump" or "circle back"
- **Vary the angle** ‚Äî each follow-up should feel different
- **Respect the silence** ‚Äî 3-4 touches max, then archive
- **Make it easy to say no** ‚Äî permission to close the loop
- **Keep it short** ‚Äî shorter than the original outreach

### Follow-Up Cadence by Scenario

| Scenario | Timing | Touches |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **No reply to CR** | Day 3, 7, 14, 30 | 4 max |
| **Replied then went silent** | Day 3, 7, 14 | 3 max |
| **No-show to call** | Same day, Day 2, Day 7 | 3 max |
| **Post-demo silence** | Day 2, 5, 10 | 3 max |
| **"Check back later"** | On specified date + 3 days | 2 max |

### Follow-Up Message Templates

Use these as starting points. Personalize based on context and previous exchange.

**Touch 1: Light Bump (Day 3-5)**
- "Hey [Name] ‚Äî wanted to make sure this didn't get buried. Worth a quick chat?"
- "Following up on this ‚Äî any interest in exploring?"

**Touch 2: Add Value (Day 7-14)**
- "Saw [relevant news/post about their company or industry] ‚Äî made me think of our conversation. Still relevant?"
- "Quick thought ‚Äî [one specific benefit relevant to their situation]. Worth 15 mins to see if it fits?"
- "Just helped a [similar company type] with [specific outcome]. Similar situation to yours. Interested?"

**Touch 3: Different Angle (Day 14-21)**
- "Different approach ‚Äî instead of a call, want to just try it? 2-week free trial: [link]"
- "Wondering if timing is the issue ‚Äî is this something to revisit in Q[X]?"
- "Should I be talking to someone else on your team about this?"

**Touch 4: Breakup Message (Day 30)**
- "Last ping on this ‚Äî figured I'd check before I close the loop. No worries either way."
- "Going to assume the timing isn't right. Happy to reconnect down the road if things change."
- "Closing the loop on this ‚Äî feel free to reach out if it becomes relevant later."

### Channel Choice for Follow-Ups

| Original Channel | Follow-Up Channel | Notes |
|------------------|-------------------|-------|
| LinkedIn DM | LinkedIn DM | Stay in-channel unless no activity |
| Email | Email | Keep thread, don't start new |
| InMail | LinkedIn DM | InMail expensive, switch to DM if connected |

### When to Archive vs. Keep Nurturing

**‚úì Archive (stop all follow-up):**
- Unsubscribe request or "remove me"
- Hostile response
- Not a fit (B2C, wrong industry, job seeker)
- 4 touches with no response
- Breakup message sent

**‚Üª Move to Long-Term Nurture (quarterly touch):**
- Good fit but wrong timing ("not right now", "check back in Q[X]")
- Using competitor (check back in 90 days)
- Pre-launch startup (follow up after launch)
- Budget constraints mentioned

**üî• Keep Active (tighter follow-up):**
- Showed strong interest then went silent
- No-show to booked call (assume good intent)
- Asked about pricing/timeline then ghosted
- Referral from existing customer

### Follow-Up Mistakes to Avoid

**‚ùå Don't do this:**
- "Just bumping this" ‚Äî Adds no value. Feels lazy.
- "Circling back" ‚Äî Overused. Everyone says this.
- "I know you're busy, but..." ‚Äî Apologetic tone undermines confidence.
- "Did you get my last message?" ‚Äî Yes, they did. They chose not to respond.
- "Following up for the Nth time" ‚Äî Makes you sound desperate.
- Re-sending the same message ‚Äî Each touch should be different.
- Following up same day ‚Äî Give them time. 3 days minimum.
- Guilt-tripping ‚Äî "I've reached out several times..." Never.
